## 1. The Concept, Refined

**Consulting Club** is a recurring, low-stakes practice arena where experienced professionals exercise consulting muscles they don't get to use in their day jobs.

The format is simple and repeatable. Each month the group convenes for a single, structured session:

- **One member plays the Client**, bringing a real or realistic problem, challenge, or opportunity to the table.
- **The remaining members form an ad hoc Consulting Team** and deliver a ~90-minute engagement: scoping the problem, structuring an approach, working the analysis, and presenting a recommendation.
- **A 15-minute debrief** closes the session — capturing what was learned, what worked, what didn't, and culminating in a candid "*Would I hire these people?*" round.

The deeper purpose is developmental, not transactional. The club exists so that mid-career experts can:

- **Stretch into unfamiliar territory** — practicing diagnosis, framing, and persuasion in a domain that isn't their home turf.
- **Take real swings with zero consequences** — failure here costs nothing and teaches everything.
- **Have fun and build relationships** with peers who are leveling up with you.
- **Test-drive a possible pivot** — for some, this is a no-risk way to find out whether consulting (or fractional/advisory work) is a career path worth pursuing.
- 
**The market thesis.** A large cohort of professionals — call them the 1990s college-grad generation — is now reaching the stage where they're rich in expertise, increasingly curious about what's next, and drawn to encore careers, fractional work, and advisory roles. Consulting Club speaks to that moment twice over: as a worthwhile activity in its own right, *and* as a safe on-ramp to exploring a new chapter.

**A note on lineage.** The facilitated peer-group model — a trusted room, a recurring cadence, structured candor, and the discipline of being accountable to your peers — is already proven at scale in the groups like Vistage, C12, YPO, and beyond. Consulting Club swaps the "share your own numbers" mechanic for a "solve someone else's problem" mechanic. That's a meaningful design choice, and gives members a competitive advantage: it's lower-vulnerability to enter (you're not exposing your own business) but produces real, gradeable skill signal.

## 2. Growth & Expansion Opportunities + Business Model

### 2.1 Two roads, and an honest tension

There are broadly two directions this can grow, and they pull against each other:

- **The intimate road** — stays small, high-trust, community-first. A network of chapters that feel like a great dinner club. Sustainable, meaningful, modest revenue.
- **The scalable road** — productizes the format, the content, and the brand into something that can serve thousands. Bigger reach, bigger revenue, but trades some of the intimacy that makes the original thing good.

Worth naming the tension up front because the business-model choices below sit on different points of that spectrum, and the "right" answer depends on what you actually want this to be.

### 2.2 Expansion vectors

**a) More chapters (horizontal).** The core unit is a chapter of 5–8 people meeting monthly. Growth = more chapters. The question is whether chapters are self-organizing (you provide the playbook and library, they run themselves) or facilitated (a trained leader runs the room). Facilitation raises quality and raises cost.

**b) College & university partnerships (the idea you floated).** Strong mutual-education play:
- *Students* bring fresh frameworks, energy, and genuine "client" problems (their own ventures, capstone projects, case competitions).
- *Alumni/professionals* bring judgment, pattern recognition, and war stories.
- *The school's advancement/alumni office* gets a high-engagement alumni touchpoint that isn't just a fundraising ask.
- A natural pilot: pair one professional chapter with one student chapter for a semester, alternating who plays Client.

**c) Corporate & L&D.** Companies pay for structured skill-building. A "Consulting Club in a box" for internal talent development — strategy practice for high-potential employees, cross-functional problem-solving reps — is a credible B2B product.

**d) Professional associations & affinity groups.** Industry associations, alumni networks, and faith/community organizations are pre-assembled audiences who could adopt the format.

**e) Career-transition / fractional on-ramp.** Lean into the "test-drive a pivot" angle as its own offering — a cohort program for people seriously evaluating a move into consulting or advisory work, with the club sessions as the lab.

**f) Facilitator training & certification.** As chapters multiply, the people who run good rooms become the scarce resource. Training and certifying facilitators is both a quality lever and a revenue line.

**g) The content library as a standalone product.** (See Section 3.) Even people who never join a chapter might pay for a well-curated, exercise-ready library of frameworks and session kits.

**h) Events & competition.** Regional gatherings, an annual "case-off," demo days where chapters present to real founders. Builds brand, community, and a sponsorship surface.

### 2.3 Candidate revenue models (mix and match)

| Model | What it is | Best fit |
|---|---|---|
| **Membership dues** | Individuals pay monthly/annual to belong to a chapter | Intimate road; predictable base revenue |
| **Freemium library** | Free core content; premium tier for the full exercise/kit library | Scalable road; low marginal cost |
| **Chapter license** | A "franchise-lite" fee + playbook + library access for self-run chapters | Horizontal scale without heavy ops |
| **B2B / corporate** | Licensed program or facilitated cohorts for internal teams | Highest revenue per deal |
| **University partnership** | Sponsored or co-funded campus chapters; advancement-office budget | Mission + mutual education |
| **Facilitator certification** | Paid training to run chapters at quality | Quality control that also pays |
| **Sponsorship** | Consulting firms / fractional platforms sponsor in exchange for recruiting access | Be careful — can warp the culture |

A plausible **starting stack**: a free or low-cost local chapter to prove the format and seed word-of-mouth, a freemium library to build an audience beyond your immediate circle, and one university pilot to validate the partnership thesis. Layer in B2B and certification only once the format is genuinely repeatable in others' hands.

> ⚠️ **Sponsorship caution.** Recruiting sponsorship is the easiest money and the fastest way to change what the room is *for*. The moment members suspect they're being evaluated for hire, the "zero-stakes" magic evaporates. Worth deciding early where the line is.

## 3. Pre-Populating the Library

The goal is a library that's **exercise-ready**, not encyclopedic — every entry should be runnable in a 90-minute session, not just read about. A good organizing spine is the **arc of an engagement**, so members learn frameworks in the order they'd actually use them, plus a kit of reusable session artifacts.

### 3.1 Phase 1 — Scoping & Diagnosis
*Getting to the real problem before solving the wrong one.*
- **Problem framing / "the question behind the question"**
- **Double-Diamond** divergent/convergent // divergent/convergent
- **Issue trees & MECE** (mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive)
- **Hypothesis-driven approach** (start with an answer, then test it)
- **Stakeholder mapping**
- **Root-cause tools** — 5 Whys, Ishikawa/fishbone
- **Scoping the engagement** — objectives, deliverables, what's out of scope

### 3.2 Phase 2 — Analysis & Frameworks
*The classic toolkit for making sense of a situation.*
- **SWOT** (the warm-up everyone knows)
- **Porter's Five Forces** — industry attractiveness
- **PESTEL** — macro environment scan
- **Value chain analysis**
- **BCG growth-share matrix** & **GE/McKinsey nine-box**
- **Ansoff matrix** — growth options
- **McKinsey 7S** — organizational alignment
- **Marketing mix (4Ps / 7Ps)** & **Jobs-to-be-Done**
- **Profit/profitability tree** — decomposing revenue and cost
- **Market sizing & estimation** (the case-interview classic; great pure-reps exercise)

### 3.3 Phase 3 — Strategy & Planning Methodologies
*Moving from analysis to a coherent direction.*
- **OKRs** (Objectives & Key Results)
- **Balanced Scorecard**
- **OGSM** (Objectives, Goals, Strategies, Measures)
- **Blue Ocean Strategy** — value innovation
- **Three Horizons** — balancing now vs. next
- **Scenario planning**
- **Wardley mapping** — situational awareness over time
- **Business Model Canvas**

### 3.4 Phase 4 — Execution, Process & Change
*Because a recommendation no one can implement is worthless.*
- **RACI** — roles and accountability
- **Kotter's 8 Steps** & **ADKAR** — change management
- **Lean / value-stream mapping** & **Six Sigma (DMAIC)** basics
- **Prioritization** — impact/effort matrix, MoSCoW, weighted scoring

### 3.5 Phase 5 — Synthesis & Communication
*Often the difference between a good team and one you'd hire.*
- **The Pyramid Principle (Minto)** — answer first, structured support
- **Executive-summary discipline** — the one-slide / one-page recommendation
- **Storyboarding the deck** before building it
- **The "so what?" test** — pushing every finding to an implication

### 3.6 Ready-to-Run Session Kit
*The artifacts that make a session actually happen.*
- **Client brief template** — for the member playing Client to prep a clean problem statement
- **Role cards** — Client, Lead Consultant, Analyst, Skeptic, Scribe (rotating)
- **A library of starter cases** — sanitized, varied across industry and problem type, so a chapter never lacks material
- **Timekeeper / agenda card** — the 90-minute structure broken into beats
- **Debrief & "Would I Hire You?" scorecard** — a light rubric (structure, insight, communication, teaming, presence) that turns the closing round into repeatable signal
- **Facilitator's guide** — how to run a great room, common failure modes, how to keep it safe and candid at once

> Suggested launch slice: don't build all of this. Ship **one framework per phase** plus the **full session kit**, run three sessions, and let what's missing reveal itself.
